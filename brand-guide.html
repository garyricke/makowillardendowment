BRAND
BRIEF

Creative North Star: The Stratospheric Command

This design system is engineered to evoke the precision, heroism, and vastness of high-altitude aviation. Unlike standard military themes that lean on heavy borders and flat olive drabs, this system takes a High-End Editorial approach — embracing the "Edge of Space" aesthetic where deep atmospheric blues meet the harsh, high-contrast light of the sun at 70,000 feet.

The layout philosophy is defined by intentional asymmetry and tonal depth. We treat the screen not as a flat canvas, but as a flight deck instrument panel overlaid against a horizon. Expect wide breathing room, overlapping elements that suggest motion, and a typographic scale that commands authority without decoration.

This endowment campaign translates a 20-year legacy of precision and service into a permanent educational institution — honoring a Cedar Rapids Kennedy High School graduate who flew to the edge of space in service of his nation, and now returns that legacy to the community that made him.

Color Implementation Rules

RULE The No-Line Rule: 1px solid borders are prohibited for sectioning. Structural boundaries must be defined through background color shifts alone.
RULE Glass & Gradient: Navigation and modals use Glassmorphism — surface colors at 60–80% opacity with 20px backdrop-blur to replicate the cockpit canopy effect.
RULE No Pure Black: Never use #000000. Always use Surface (#0b1326) or Surface Lowest (#060e20) to maintain the "Deep Space" atmospheric depth.
RULE Ghost Borders Only: If a container requires definition, use the outline token at maximum 15% opacity — a "glint" on the edge, never a hard line.
RULE Dragon Red is Singular: One Dragon Red CTA per page — the "ejection handle." Overuse destroys its urgency as a conversion signal.
RULE Atmospheric Shadows: For floating elements, use ambient shadows tinted with primary (#b6c4ff at 8% opacity) with blur values of 32px+ — mimicking scattered high-altitude light.

DO

  • Use high-altitude imagery showing the curvature of the Earth and the edge of the atmosphere
  • Show aurora borealis, stratospheric sunsets, and atmospheric phenomena as witnessed from the U-2 cockpit
  • Apply deep navy-to-transparent gradient overlays when placing copy over photographs
  • Prefer wide, landscape-oriented images that emphasize the horizon and vast open space
  • Use Lt. Col. Willard's official military portrait for formal contexts and editorial headers
  • Show authentic flight line and aircraft imagery — U-2, T-38, B-1B — in their operational context

DON'T

  • Use stock photography, generic military clipart, or AI-generated aerospace imagery
  • Apply heavy filters, oversaturation, or Instagram-style color grades to mission photography
  • Place campaign copy directly on unmodified photographs without an overlay treatment
  • Use images below 2000px on the long edge — resolution is a non-negotiable quality signal
  • Crop the aircraft out of aviation photography — the airframe is the operational context
  • Mix casual personal photos with formal campaign copy — the register must match throughout
06 Voice & Tone
LANGUAGE
OF ALTITUDE

Campaign language must be evocative, deeply respectful of military service, and action-oriented. We speak with command, not bureaucracy. We earn every word.

▲

Authoritative

Write with command. Passive voice is grounded. This campaign speaks from decades of real achievement — active verbs, present tense where possible, declarative sentences that land like a landing gear on a bicycle-gear aircraft.

◎

Evocative

The best campaign copy makes you feel 70,000 feet of altitude. Ground technical data in human experience. Translate flight hours into sacrifice. Translate the endowment into a Kennedy student's future.

◈

Precise

We do not exaggerate. We do not need to. The career record of Lt. Col. Willard speaks for itself. Every statistic cited must be verifiable. Accuracy is the brand's core promise.

⊕

Rooted

Cedar Rapids. Kennedy High School. The Iowa Hawkeyes. This campaign began on these grounds and returns to them. Never let national scale erase the local pride that is the entire point.

◆

Action-Oriented

Every section should drive to a decision. "Donate Today." "Apply Now." "Secure the Legacy." We are on a launch clock — urgency is not manufactured, it is real. The Fini Flight is April 9th.

○

Respectful

We honor genuine military sacrifice. We do not sensationalize, mythologize, or exploit service. The language honors the man by being worthy of the mission. Dignity is non-negotiable.

COPY EXAMPLES — WRONG VS. RIGHT
× Wrong — Generic & Passive
"We are collecting donations for a scholarship fund to help students at Kennedy High School in Cedar Rapids. Please consider giving whatever you can to support this worthy cause."
✓ Right — Mission Voice
"From the edge of space to the heart of Iowa — join us in making Lt. Col. Willard's legacy permanent. Every dollar endows a future engineer, aviator, or leader in the halls where he first learned to aim high."
× Wrong — Vague & Unearned
"Dustin Willard had a really impressive career in the military. He flew some really important planes and did a lot of important missions over his 20 years of service."
✓ Right — Precise & Earned
"Logging 3,000 flight hours — over half in active combat — Lt. Col. Willard piloted the B-1B Lancer, commanded MC-12W reconnaissance, and mastered the U-2S Dragon Lady at 70,000 feet. The record speaks."

"Donations are tax-deductible pursuant to 501(c)(3) status. The Cedar Rapids Community Schools Foundation will administer the fund per UPMIFA guidelines applicable to Iowa fiduciaries."
✓ Right — Transparent but Human
"Your gift is fully tax-deductible, administered in perpetuity by the Cedar Rapids Community Schools Foundation — the same trusted institution that has funded Kennedy scholarships for decades."
07 Rules of Engagement
MISSION
PARAMETERS

The design system lives or dies by its rules. These are not suggestions — they are the parameters of the mission.

DO

  • 01
    Use asymmetrical layouts Place a large headline on the left and leave the right 40% open. The empty space is the altitude — it is not a mistake, it is the design.
  • 02
    Use photography as atmospheric texture Stratospheric images used as background elements, obscured by deep blue overlays at 15–30% opacity. The image creates mood; it is not the subject.
  • 03
    Use 0.25rem (sm) border radius throughout It feels more instrumental and tactical than overly rounded shapes. Soft corners read consumer; sharp corners read precision engineering.
  • 04
    Use B612 Mono for all quantitative data Flight hours, donation amounts, funding percentages, dates, hex codes — if it's a number or a technical identifier, it belongs in the cockpit font.
  • 05
    Allow 80px of vertical whitespace between sections No dividers. Breathing room between content blocks is the structural grammar of this system. White space communicates altitude.
  • 06
    Match photography register to copy register Formal campaign sections use the official uniform portrait. Context sections use flight line imagery. The visual and verbal tone must travel together.

DON'T

  • 01
    Use pure black (#000000) Always use Surface (#0b1326) or Surface Lowest (#060e20). Pure black breaks atmospheric immersion — we exist in deep space, not a void.
  • 02
    Use 1px solid borders for sectioning The No-Line Rule is fundamental. Section boundaries must emerge from background color shifts. If you're drawing a divider, redesign the layout.
  • 03
    Use standard "Success Green" for UI states Use Primary (#b6c4ff) for success states and Tactical Gold for warnings. Cougar Green is reserved for brand identity — not component state management.
  • 04
    Use 100% opaque borders anywhere If a ghost border is truly required, maximum 15% opacity on the outline token. A "glint" — never a hard line. Hard lines shatter atmospheric depth.
  • 05
    Deploy Dragon Red (#EE263E) more than once per page One red button per page — the ejection handle. Pull it too often and it loses all urgency. Red is reserved for the single primary conversion goal.
  • 06
    Use generic military stock photography This campaign's photography is real, personal, and historically significant. No iStock fighter jets. No Shutterstock eagles. No AI-generated cockpits. Authentic only.
